Lauren Torrance

Fuck Life

A hybrid book/art project that leans into urgency and humor. Lauren pairs visceral prose with tactile layers of paint, collage, and charcoal to map the chaos and tenderness of living through late-night creativity.

Back to Visual Art

Project overview

“Fuck Life” is equal parts diary and gallery installation. The work stitches together 80+ pages of handwritten narrative with a series of companion paintings. Each spread is meant to be read aloud, set to the soundtrack that the writing was born from—sparse synths, field recordings, and layered vocals.

The title is a wink at the messy, beautiful contradictions of creative work, not a manifesto of despair. It asks visitors to sit with discomfort and delight at the same time.

Creative process

Writing

Lauren drafts in a notebook, then scans pages to preserve texture. The text is layered with annotations that describe the soundscapes she hears while working.

Visuals

The paint studies begin with washes of graphite and ink, building toward saturated pigments. Each canvas is photographed, printed, and reworked to become part of the book’s physical spread.

Recording

A portable setup captures ambient noise, vocals, and guitar loops. These audio fragments appear as QR codes in the book, letting readers unlock companion tracks.
